Morning all. Happy Easter weekend! Here's what I've been reading and thinking about this week. It's pretty business and startup-heavy this week, so if that doesn't interest you, just check back in next week.

### ‍**Books**

- [Berkshire Hathaway Letters to Shareholders:](http://amzn.to/22sDfnw) I was reading this last week, and I'm still going through it. This is every letter that Warren Buffett wrote to shareholders from 1965 to 2015 -- I'm up to about 1985 right now. Buffett has so many witty stories and aphorisms that it would be a fun project to collect them and put them in a book. An idea for later.
- [Buffett: The Making of an American Capitalist by Roger Lowenstein](http://amzn.to/22C6V5f): obviously reading his essays made me want to read more of his history and backstory. With guys like Buffett, I'm always much more interested in their origin stories. The first million, not the first billion. Great read.
- [#AskGaryVee: One Entrepreneur's Take on Leadership, Social Media, and Self-Awareness by Gary Vaynerchuk](http://amzn.to/1Pwz9BM): Gary's a great inspirational and motivational guy, and this book is a good collection of his answers to questions that people asked him on his Youtube channel. I like it -- you can feel his energy and enthusiasm coming off the page.

### **Links**

- [Serial entrepreneur Jay Samit on the SellPersonal podcast](https://sellpersonal.com/ep32/): I'd honestly never heard of Jay Samit until I listened to this interview, but I was blown away. He clearly and concisely laid out how you can look around for problems that need to be solved, and go solve them, and build great companies in the process. I also picked up his book [Disrupt You!](http://amzn.to/1pOlfX4) after I'd listened to this interview.
- [The only entrepreneurship reading list you need](http://tuckermax.me/the-only-entrepreneurship-lesson-reading-list-you-need-asshole-to-ceo-3/): my old boss Tucker Max continued his Asshole to CEO series with a fantastic reading list on startups and entrepreneurship. I'll give you the Cliff Notes: Paul Graham, good, Guy Kawasaki, bad.
- [Paul Graham on how to get startup ideas](http://paulgraham.com/startupideas.html): Tucker's list sent me down a bit of a rabbit hole on Paul Graham's essays, but this is one of the best. Just like Jay Samit said: look for problems that need solving.
- [The Marc Andreessen Guide to Startups](http://pmarchive.com/guide_to_startups_part1.html): again, one from Tucker's list -- I hadn't seen these essays before, but Marc Andreessen (Netscape, Opsware, Ning, Andreessen Horowitz) is basically the best there is when it comes to talking about tech and startups. He talks from a place of huge experience, and this is a great read. Pretty short too.
- [90 Day Goal Setting and Action Step Planning Template](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1qGS1XmIgm8csCr5toAmB4J9OYc9G_pKx0BDP7lSOqWg/edit): this is one of the resources from my friend [Taylor Pearson](http://www.taylorpearson.me/)'s book. Hugely useful and practical way to break down your long-term goals into 90 day steps, then monthly, weekly and daily. I'll definitely be using this.
- [10 Habits of Unsuccessful People You Don't Want to Copy](https://medium.com/life-learning/10-habits-of-unsuccessful-people-you-don-t-want-to-copy-48c429b7947a): Taleb would call this "via negativa". Munger would call it "inverting the problem". Whatever you want to call it, you can really progress by just trying to avoid making mistakes. Here are 10 habits you should stay away from.
